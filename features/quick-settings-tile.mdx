---
title: "Quick Settings Tile"
description: "Add OpenCalc to the Android Quick Settings panel for one-tap access from anywhere, including the lock screen."
---

OpenCalc can be added to Android's Quick Settings panel — the row of tiles that appears when you swipe down from the top of your screen. Once added, the tile opens the calculator instantly from anywhere on the device.

---

## Adding the tile

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Quick Settings panel">
    Swipe down from the top of your screen, then swipe down again to expand the full Quick Settings panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter edit mode">
    Tap the **pencil (edit) icon** at the bottom of the panel. The exact position varies by device manufacturer, but it is typically in the lower-left or lower-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the OpenCalc tile">
    Scroll through the list of available tiles until you see **OpenCalc**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drag it into the active area">
    Drag the OpenCalc tile up into the active tile section. Release to place it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Exit edit mode">
    Tap the back button or **Done** to exit edit mode.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The steps above describe stock Android. Samsung One UI, Xiaomi MIUI, and other manufacturer skins may show the edit mode differently, but the general process of long-pressing or tapping an edit icon is the same.
</Note>

---

## Using the tile

Tap the OpenCalc tile in Quick Settings to launch the app. The panel collapses and OpenCalc opens immediately.

- On **Android 14 (Upside Down Cake) and higher**, the tile uses `startActivityAndCollapse` with a `PendingIntent`, which is the required API on newer versions.
- On **Android 13 and below**, the tile calls `startActivity` with `FLAG_ACTIVITY_NEW_TASK` directly.

In both cases, the behavior from your perspective is the same: one tap opens OpenCalc.

---

## Lock screen access

OpenCalc can be displayed over the lock screen, so you can open it from the Quick Settings tile without unlocking your device first.

This is controlled by the **Show on lock screen** setting (enabled by default). When active, OpenCalc sets the `SHOW_WHEN_LOCKED` and `TURN_SCREEN_ON` flags so the app appears above the lock screen.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Android 8.1+">
    Uses `setShowWhenLocked(true)` and `setTurnScreenOn(true)` — the modern API introduced in API 27.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Android 8.0 and below">
    Uses the legacy `FLAG_SHOW_WHEN_LOCKED` and `FLAG_TURN_SCREEN_ON` window flags.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

To disable this behavior, go to **Settings → Show on lock screen** and turn it off.

<Warning>
  When the app is shown over the lock screen, anyone with physical access to the device can open the calculator without entering a PIN or biometric. If this is a concern, disable the setting.
</Warning>
