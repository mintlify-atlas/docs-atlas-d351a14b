OpenCalc ships with four visual styles. The selected theme is applied immediately — no app restart is required.

---

## Changing the theme

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the menu">
    Tap the three-dot menu button in the top-right corner of the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Select **Settings** from the dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Theme">
    Tap the **Theme** preference to open the theme selector dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a theme">
    Choose your preferred style. The app reloads immediately with the new theme applied.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available themes

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="System" icon="sun-moon">
    Follows the device's day/night mode setting. On Android 12+, automatically uses Material You if dynamic colors are available. On older devices, falls back to the default light/dark theme.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Light" icon="sun">
    Forces the app to use a light background regardless of the system setting.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dark" icon="moon">
    Forces a dark background regardless of the system setting.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Amoled" icon="circle-dot">
    Pure black (`#000000`) background. Saves battery on OLED and AMOLED screens because black pixels are turned off completely.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Material You (Android 12+)

Material You generates a color palette from your current wallpaper and applies it across all system apps. When **System** is selected on a device running Android 12 or higher, OpenCalc automatically uses this dynamic palette.

<Note>
  Material You is only available when your device supports dynamic colors (`DynamicColors.isDynamicColorAvailable()` returns `true`). On older devices or devices without wallpaper-based theming, the System option falls back to the standard light/dark theme.
</Note>

The theme selector dialog labels the System option as **System (Material You)** when dynamic colors are available, so you can confirm at a glance whether it is active on your device.

---

## Amoled theme

The Amoled theme sets the background to pure black and forces night mode. It is a good choice if:

- Your phone has an OLED or AMOLED screen (most modern flagship and mid-range Android phones).
- You frequently use the calculator in dark environments.
- You want to maximize battery savings while using the app.

<Tip>
  On LCD screens, Amoled appears as a very dark theme rather than offering power savings, but it remains usable as a high-contrast dark option.
</Tip>

---

## Theme reload behavior

When you select a new theme, OpenCalc calls `finish()` on the current activity and immediately relaunches it with the new theme applied. Your current input and history are preserved — only the visual style changes.
