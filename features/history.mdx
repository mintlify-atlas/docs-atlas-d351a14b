---
title: "Calculation History"
description: "View, restore, and manage your past calculations in OpenCalc's history panel."
---

OpenCalc records your calculations in a scrollable panel that slides up from the bottom of the screen. History persists across app restarts and is stored locally on your device.

---

## Opening the history panel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the history button">
    Tap the arrow button at the bottom of the screen to slide the history panel up.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Or swipe up from the bottom">
    You can also swipe up from the bottom edge of the calculator to reveal the panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dismiss with the back gesture or button">
    Swipe down or press the system back button to collapse the panel without leaving the app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## What each history entry stores

Every entry records three pieces of information:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Expression** | The full calculation as it appeared in the input field (e.g., `12 × (3 + 4)`) |
| **Result** | The computed answer at the time the entry was saved (e.g., `84`) |
| **Timestamp** | The time the calculation was recorded, stored as a Unix millisecond epoch |

---

## Restoring a past calculation

Tap any history entry to load its **result** into the main input field. You can then continue building on that value — for example, tap an entry showing `84` and immediately type `+ 16` to continue.

---

## Deleting history entries

### Swipe to delete

Swipe any history entry left or right to delete it. This behavior can be turned off in Settings → **Delete history entries by swiping** (enabled by default).

### Clear all history

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the app menu">
    Tap the three-dot menu button in the top-right corner of the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Clear history">
    Select **Clear history** from the menu. All entries are removed immediately from both the panel and persistent storage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Clearing history is permanent. There is no undo.
</Warning>

---

## Auto-save while typing

When **Auto-save calculation without equal button** is enabled (the default), OpenCalc saves your in-progress calculation to history as you type — you do not need to press **=**.

The behavior is designed to avoid clutter:

- A new history entry is created when you start a new calculation.
- As you continue editing the **same** calculation, the existing entry is updated in place (expression, result, and timestamp are refreshed).
- When you start an entirely new calculation, a fresh entry is created.

To disable this and only save on **=**, turn off **Auto-save calculation without equal button** in Settings.

---

## History size limit

The maximum number of stored entries is configurable in Settings → **History size** (default: **50**).

| Setting value | Behavior |
|---|---|
| A positive number (e.g., 50) | Oldest entries are removed when the limit is exceeded |
| **Unlimited** (-1 internally) | All entries are kept indefinitely |
| **0 / Disabled** | History is disabled entirely; the panel button is hidden |

<Note>
  When you reduce the history size below the current number of stored entries, OpenCalc immediately trims the oldest entries to fit the new limit.
</Note>

---

## Persistence

History is serialized to JSON using Gson and stored in Android's `SharedPreferences`. Entries survive:

- App restarts
- Device reboots
- Screen rotation

History is **not** synced to the cloud and remains local to the device.

---

## When history is disabled

If the history size is set to **0**, the history button is hidden and the sliding panel is disabled. No calculations are saved, and tapping **=** or typing does not trigger any storage writes.
