OpenCalc is a free, open-source Android calculator designed to be fast, private, and pleasant to use. With no ads, no unnecessary permissions, and a custom-built math engine, it's a clean alternative to the bloated calculators that ship on most Android devices.

## What makes OpenCalc different

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Lightweight (~2MB)" icon="feather">
    The entire app fits in about 2MB — no bloat, no tracking, no ads.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Custom math engine" icon="calculator">
    Built-in algebra parser handles complex expressions without third-party dependencies.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multiple themes" icon="palette">
    Choose from Light, Dark, Amoled, and Material You to match your style.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Privacy first" icon="shield">
    No internet permission. No data collection. Minimal permissions — only what's needed for haptics and lock screen.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Explore the docs

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the app">
    Get OpenCalc from [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.darkempire78.opencalculator), [F-Droid](https://f-droid.org/en/packages/com.darkempire78.opencalculator), or [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/Darkempire78/OpenCalc/releases/latest).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Learn the basics">
    Read the [Quick Start guide](/quickstart) to understand the interface, basic operations, and how to navigate the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable scientific mode">
    Tap the expand button to reveal [scientific functions](/features/scientific-mode) — sin, cos, tan, ln, log, sqrt, and more.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize to your preference">
    Visit [Settings](/settings/overview) to adjust the theme, calculation precision, history behavior, and other options.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  OpenCalc requires Android 5.0 (API level 21) or higher. The app is fully open source under the [GPL-3.0 license](https://github.com/Darkempire78/OpenCalc/blob/main/LICENSE).
</Note>
